9 simple rules for dressing when fat
by Miriam Zisook
Nobody wants to look fat, but there are so many myths out there about how to dress your best. Here we wade through the myths to share 9 tried and true tips for dressing to fit and flatter. Look 10 pounds thinner by tonight if you follow these wardrobe tricks.
Most women, especially fat* women, are taught that the paramount rule of getting dressed is to flatter our figures. We keep a list of rules in our minds against which to evaluate every possible outfit. Is it too tight? Too bold? Too exposing? When we see beautiful clothes we think, 'maybe when we lose weight.' When we see something beautiful on another woman's body we think, "I could never pull that off." When following the standard rules set by society, choosing clothing sure is boring and fills us with self loathing. Here's a new list of fashion rules, to present ourselves with joy, to play with new ways to express ourselves, to be comfortable in our own skins, and and to raise a big fat middle finger to the patriarchy.
1. Streamline With (mono) Color, especially black
Glamour: How to Dress 10 Pounds Thinner
"Look taller and trimmer by dressing monochromatically head to toe. "Lose" five pounds by matching your pants or skirt, stockings and shoes. You'll seem 10 pounds lighter if they're all black! Bright colors and prints will call unnecessary attention to your shape and make you look bigger. Is the one-color thing too tame for your taste? Slip on a pair of bright shoes to shake things up."
Selena Gomez dressed head to toe in black. She looks to chic and thin in these streamlined, meture looks.
1. Look Joyful in Bright Colors
Clothes aren't just meant to hide your body, they can also be for fun and decoration. When you wear unique and playful pieces, especially handmade by you, you won't just see clothing as camouflage, you'll hope to get noticed. There's nothing better than answering a compliment with, "Thanks, I made it!"

2. Go for a midi-length skirt
Who What Wear: 30 Summer Outfits That Make Everyone Look 10 Pounds Thinner
"If your legs are one of your worry zones, you’re in luck! Midi-length skirts are perennially in style, and the combination of extra fabric and a cinch at the waist never fail to flatter your figure. Go for a hemline that hits below the kneecap to make legs look their absolute best."
The best way to make your legs look thin is to hide them! Jessica Alba looks slender and sophisticated in this midi length skirt.
2. Don't let anyone else's opinions be the boss of you
When you think of wearing a short skirt maybe, like me, you think of that time in college when you were out to lunch with a man (ahem, dad) and asked him to stop leering at that short skirt-wearing waitress. Maybe that man (ahem, dad) retorted, "You're just jealous because you can't wear a skirt like that." No? That didn’t happen to you? Ok, well whatever your version of the too-short skirt story is, maybe it gave you the idea you'd look disgusting and be de-sexualized in a too short skirt. Maybe that made you realize that if you were lucky enough not to be desexualized, the only other option was to be leered at and hyper-sexualized. Not great options. Put on a short skirt and don't give those gross sexist comments any more power for even one more minute.

3. Skin Tight Dresses—Might Not Be A Great Idea
10 Styles that Make Women Look Fat
"Wearing a dress that's skin-tight is a big mistake if you want to look thinner and taller. If a dress feels tight, if horizontal wrinkles are appearing (especially across the bust, hips, or thighs), or if lumps and bumps are clearly visible, the dress does not belong in your wardrobe. The too-tight dress effect is likely to be multiplied if the dress also has other unflattering features, such as being too short or too revealing. Certain fabrics can also compound the fattening effect."
Claire Foy is elegantly hidden behind a cascade of fabric in this Ocscar de la Renta red carpet look.
3. Don't give a f*ck in a tight dress
Recently I came across an online trend in the sewing community: #wiggledress. Wiggle dresses are dresses so tight you have to wiggle into them, and curvy sewists the world over are posting photos of themselves looking spectacular in them. Maybe, like I used to before I saw so many women looking so freaking fabulous in them, you think of wiggle dresses as anathema to curvy dressing. Try going way the hell out of your comfort zone and wearing one. When you wince at yourself in it, think of how beautiful those other women look in them and remind yourself the problem with your body might just be in your head (planted there by—-you guessed it—-patriarchy).

4. Avoid Horizontal Stripes
Daily Mail, Yes: Stripes Do Make you Look Fat
"For years mothers have told their daughters that horizontal stripes do the female figure no favors and that vertical ones are more flattering. As a result, many women have spent their lives avoiding horizontal hoops for fear of looking fat." "Val Watham, 53, was unconvinced by research which claimed horizontal stripes flatter the figure. She undertook her own research, dressing models in a variety of striped designs and asking members of the public to rate their appearance. The findings demonstrated that horizontal stripes do make people appear wider, while vertical stripes make people appear taller."
Beyoncé looks thin and fabulous in this jumpsuit--not because she's &*%$ Beyoncé but because of the magical optical illusion of vertical stripes.
4. Never change your stripes
For years I didn't wear horizontal stripes because my mom said they make me look fat. Oh really! Your mom too?! What a coincidence! That's the dumbest shit I've ever heard—as if someone can't tell what I look like when I wear polka dots but my body blows up like a blimp (thanks Mom for the blimp comparison) in stripes. A tiger can’t change her stripes, and neither should you! Make yourself a whole bunch of fun clothes with horizontal stripes to celebrate saying goodbye to this nonsense fashion rule.

5. Cover up leggings with a tunic
10 Styles that Make Women Look Fat
"Skinny jeans [and leggings] look best when worn by skinny women. Just because skinny jeans are available in all sizes doesn't mean that they're a flattering style choice. Jeans with straight legs almost always look better on short women (unless they wear a tiny size) than skinny jeans do."
I think we can all agree celebrity model Devon WIndsor doesn't have any junk in her trunk--but if she did we definitely couldn't see it through that elegant tunic and jacket.
5. Be extremely comfortable in leggings as pants
When leggings first started to become pants, I told myself they were only pants for thin women. Dressing for yoga and exercise meant pulling together creative outfits that hid my athleisure under tunics and skirts. I saw women all around me comfy AF in leggings, but thought that wasn't for me. One day I didn't put my skirt back on over my leggings after yoga, and guess what happened?! Nothing. Nothing happened. See for yourself by getting yourself some bold leggings (and some plain ones) that you'll absolutely love rocking as pants. Embrace that soft caress of lycra against your liberated legs.

6. Toss a light jacket over your shoulders
Who What Wear: 30 Summer Outfits That Make Everyone Look 10 Pounds Thinner
"If your problem areas tend to reside more in your breadth—in other words, your shoulders, upper arms, and back area—tossing a light jacket across your shoulders does worlds to melt off the pounds."
Actress and singer Emma Roberts elegantly hides hides her shoulders under a denim jacket.
6. Bare your arms
In 6th grade I got a new tank top I absolutely loved. The first time I wore it outside to play in the front yard with my neighbors my mom looked at me uncomfortably and suggested it would look cute under something with sleeves. I didn't wear a sleeveless top again for more than 10 years. Oh, wow! Something like that happened to you too?! You've had enough of sweating through the summer hidden under sweaters and passing up cute tops and dresses because they're for someone else's body. Say goodbye to cardigans in 90 degree heat, and say hello to dinosaur sundresses (or the playful prints of your choice)!

7. Pick Simple, unadorned denim
In Style, 12 Easy Ways to Look Thinner By Tonight
"Clean, streamlined jeans are the way to go. The most flattering styles are dark and feature unembellished pockets. Avoid extra details that catch the eye and give the illusion of bulk-think twice about tags, grommets, or decorative extras. The leanest silhouettes of all: boot cut and straight leg."
Heidi klum looking effortlessly slim in head to plain denim.
7. "Pull off" decorative prints and bold looks
When clothes are camouflage, all kinds of fun styles are off limits. I've been seeing an explosion of cute jumpsuit styles and thinking they were off limits to me. I've been seeing an explosion of fun floral prints and thinking they were off limits to me. I've been seeing printed and color denims and thinking they were off limits to me. Now I present you with this rocking floral denim jumpsuit. I dare you to pick out that too bold fashion piece and wear it out—delight in how nobody but you even thinks you're doing it because I dared you—you look great. All you need to do to pull it off is put it on!

8. Layer It Up—And Make-Believe
Style Craze: How To Dress To Hide Belly Fat
"Layering is another technique that magically hides it all away, shifting the focus anywhere but your tummy. Combinations like the tank top with plaid shirt; knee-length dress and a cape; trousers with a shirt and blazer or shrug etc. work pretty well to your advantage. Layers that are unbuttoned gives it a significant effect making you look taller. Make-believe in the truest sense."
One way to hide your fat is just ot hide your whole body! Let this look from an article called '5 Outfits to look Thinner in a Minute' show you how its done.
8. You have nothing to hide
My senior year of high school there was a day when underclassmen in stage crew dressed up as seniors. The kid who dressed as me wore tons of layers—long sleeve T under short sleeve T, flared jeans under a knee length skirt, a cardigan, and a scarf. I've thought about that a lot since then—it was so transparent how much I was hiding. Now I sometimes just wear one thing, and you too can wear just one thing! Pick out a favorite top or dress and then stop, don't add a cardigan, don't add a jacket, no need for a scarf, just take your totally dressed enough self to into the sunshine.

9. Embrace Shapewear
ABC, Fake It 'Til You Make It: Best Shapewear for Women
"You should try these if you have not already. They are your rescue rangers; I could not wait to get my hands on these. It basically, tucks the unflattering flab and tires back into the tummy, at least it creates the illusion. These soft, comfortable and tight-fitting undergarments come in a lot of variants, taking full control of your tummy area. You can easily get away with those body hugging dresses that were forever part of your wish list. It makes a lot of difference, especially if you make smart choices."
You'd better beleive Mindy Kaling wear shapewear, you can't be a "curvy" woman in hollywood without it.
9. Really, nothing at all to hide
In high school when my friends went to the beach (an experience I dreaded) I wore shapewear under jeans and said I hated swimming. Years later I found an emerald green suit that fit me and I swam at the beach with friends for the first time. Turns out, the beach is my favorite place! When I saw an online campaign declaring that every body is a bikini body, it was a revelation. Here were women claiming the right to define a good enough body. Could it be that all we need to do to have a good enough body to enjoy the beach is decide our bodies are good enough to enjoy the beach? I made myself this emerald bikini as an homage to that emerald one piece from years ago, and the first suit I ever felt comfortable in, but this time with some belly showing. Find a swimsuit you love, maybe even one that doesn't come down to your knees and elbows, maybe even one that shows a belly roll, and hit the water with nothing to hide.
