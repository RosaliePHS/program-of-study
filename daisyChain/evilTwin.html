9 Simple Rules for Dressing When Fat
9 slides
Tried and true tips to help you dress to slim and flatter
by Miriam Zisook
June 30, 2019

How you look is the first thing people see when they meet you. Women who want success in our personal and professional lives know that caring for our appearance is essential. In fact, thin women earn higher salaries than their average and overweight peers and accrue numerous personal and professional benefits: Attractive people are more likely to be hired, more likely to be perceived as healthy, more likely to be taken seriously by doctors, and more likely to be pursued romantically.

Clothes are an essential part of how we present ourselves and make those indelible first impressions. Of course, nobody wants to look fat! Even if your body has flaws, you can use your clothing to look more svelte. There are so many myths out there about how to dress your best. Here we wade through the myths to share 9 tried and true tips for dressing to fit and flatter. Look 10 pounds thinner by tonight if you follow these wardrobe tricks. The next best thing to workouts and eating right? These figure-flattering style tips.

1
Streamline With monocolor, especially black
about the outfit Selena Gomez dressed head to toe in black. She looks so chic and thin in these streamlined, mature looks.
Glamour: How to Dress 10 Pounds Thinner

Look taller and trimmer by dressing monochromatically head to toe. "Lose" five pounds by matching your pants or skirt, stockings and shoes. You'll seem ten pounds lighter if they're all black! Bright colors and prints will call unnecessary attention to your shape and make you look bigger. Is the one-color thing too tame for your taste? Slip on a pair of bright shoes to shake things up.

Go for a midi-length skirt
about the outfit The best way to make your legs look thin is to hide them! Jessica Alba looks slender and sophisticated in this midi length skirt.
Who What Wear: 30 Summer Outfits That Make Everyone Look 10 Pounds Thinner

If your legs are one of your worry zones, you’re in luck! Midi-length skirts are perennially in style, and the combination of extra fabric and a cinch at the waist never fail to flatter your figure. Go for a hemline that hits below the kneecap to make legs look their absolute best.

3
Skin Tight Dresses—Might Not Be A Great Idea
about the outfit Claire Foy is elegantly hidden behind a cascade of fabric in this Ocscar de la Renta red carpet look.
3
10 Styles that Make Women Look Fat

Wearing a dress that's skin-tight is a big mistake if you want to look thinner and taller. If a dress feels tight, if horizontal wrinkles are appearing (especially across the bust, hips, or thighs), or if lumps and bumps are clearly visible, the dress does not belong in your wardrobe. The too-tight dress effect is likely to be multiplied if the dress also has other unflattering features, such as being too short or too revealing. Certain fabrics can also compound the fattening effect.

Avoid Horizontal Stripes
about the outfit Beyoncé looks thin and fabulous in this jumpsuit--not because she's &*%$ Beyoncé, but because of the magical optical illusion of vertical stripes.
Daily Mail, Yes: Stripes Do Make you Look Fat

For years mothers have told their daughters that horizontal stripes do the female figure no favors and that vertical ones are more flattering. As a result, many women have spent their lives avoiding horizontal hoops for fear of looking fat." "Val Watham, 53, was unconvinced by research which claimed horizontal stripes flatter the figure. She undertook her own research, dressing models in a variety of striped designs and asking members of the public to rate their appearance. The findings demonstrated that horizontal stripes do make people appear wider, while vertical stripes make people appear taller.

Toss a light jacket over your shoulders

If your problem areas tend to reside more in your breadth—in other words, your shoulders, upper arms, and back area—tossing a light jacket across your shoulders does worlds to melt off the pounds.

Pick Simple, unadorned denim

Clean, streamlined jeans are the way to go. The most flattering styles are dark and feature unembellished pockets. Avoid extra details that catch the eye and give the illusion of bulk-think twice about tags, grommets, or decorative extras. The leanest silhouettes of all: boot cut and straight leg.

Combinations like the tank top with plaid shirt; knee-length dress and a cape; trousers with a shirt and blazer or shrug etc. work pretty well to your advantage. Layers that are unbuttoned gives it a significant effect making you look taller. Make-believe in the truest sense.
