Why are you doing it?
When I was reading through the emails something that stuck out to me was that all of my mom’s behavior is fueled by a desperate wish to keep me close to her. She’ll say and do just about anything if she thinks it has a chance of getting my attention, and she switches approach quickly as she exhausts her options. 

I also saw in my responses to her that I have a set of strategies too, and I lose my composure and say impulsive and unkind things occasionally too. Almost always, I took time, asked other people for advice, composed messages painstakingly, analyzed when was the right time of day to send them, how long to wait, who to run them by before hitting send. I kept drafts for hours before sending, just in case I changed my mind. 

While she and I are both using strategies, the way we play is extremely different. Where I was careful, cautious, and  strategic she was impulsive and emotional. Eventually, coming to the decision to cut off communication was largely realizing we weren’t playing the same game, and I was never going to be able to ask her to play by my rules, any more than I was willing to play by hers. 

As I read through the emails I created a set of qualitative codes to organize the strategies I noticed her using. The strategies I identified are:
  • Worrying :Times when she claims to be worried about me or someone else to get me to communicate
  • Enrolling: when she tries to give me information I don’t want, or to rope me in to a problem or crisis
  • Pleading: persistently asking me to call, text, visit, etc.
  • Erasing: Pretending something hasn’t happened, or changing her position without acknowledgement
  • Distracting: changing the subject, random articles, requests to visit out of nowhere
  • Repairing: more reasonable apology type emails
  • Threatening: threatening to move on without me in her life, to cut off communication, to cut off the phone plan, etc.
  • Gaslighting: Accusing me of narcissism, of making others unhappy because I am unhappy
  • Accusing: Saying mean things, scolding or reprimanding, blaming
  • Rationalizing: Explaining how her responses are reasonable under the circumstances"
  • Self-glorifying: Talking about how she is the kindest, best person, so fun and funny, so wise, etc.
  • Invalidating: Saying something isn't a problem because it doesn't bother her, telling me to just get over it

What will you wind up with?
I'd like to create a board game or card game to capture how the relationship feels based on the strategy codes. The rough idea would be a game where one person is playing a complicated and convoluted strategy game (me) and the other person is just randomly flipping over cards (her). 
  • The experience of the player playing me: They have the task of composing a message, and are going through complicated, multi-step, strategic processes involving asking friends for help, waiting for replies, waiting for random amounts of time to cool down, writing certain numbers of drafts, etc. I think they should not actually be composing a message by writing, but somehow collecting the pieces they need in card form or something
  • The experience of the player playing my mom: They get a deck of cards, shuffle it, set it down and no matter what the player being me does they just turn over the next card as their response. 
I think the demo of this should be me playing as my mom and getting to experience other people playing as me. 

What's an inspiring peer? 
I’m thinking about the genre of board games, and although I don’t actually intend to create a play-able game (the whole idea is that it is not playable, because the 2 players are playing different games) I think it will be most effective if it successfully borrows the visual language and mechanics of real games. 

How will you do it? 
  • I’m going to start by creating a draft of my “mom’s" card deck, because I know more or less what I want that to be and what I want “her” to be doing
  • Next I will play with prototypes of my deck and instructions, and review other games for ideas about how to create the game mechanics, visual language and instructions
  • Alongside this, I am considering going through the emails and coding my strategies, because I think using real text from my messages to her could be interesting.
