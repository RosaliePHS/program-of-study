When thinking about how I might “kintsugi” the emails I began to think about Splitting: 

"Splitting (also called black-and-white thinking or all-or-nothing thinking) is the failure in a person's thinking to bring together the dichotomy of both positive and negative qualities of the self and others into a cohesive, realistic whole. It is a common defense mechanism used by many people.[1] The individual tends to think in extremes (i.e., an individual's actions and motivations are all good or all bad with no middle ground).
The concept of splitting was first described by Ronald Fairbairn in his formulation of object relations theory;[2] it begins as the inability of the infant to combine the fulfilling aspects of the parents (the good object) and their unresponsive aspects (the unsatisfying object) into the same individuals, instead seeing the good and bad as separate. In psychoanalytic theory this functions as a defense mechanism.[3]”
This is a very significant theme in the emails, and one that I think is a significant theme for me in my own emotional development. Namely, it is hard for me to internalize that healthy people can hold a long and nuanced view of other people because the emotional culture I was raised in was so dichotomous. 
I think it will be meaningful for me to do a restorative process of examining the splitting in my mom’s communication with me, and finding a way to repair it by generating a more balanced set of narratives. An idea I have is to invite other people in my life to describe me in complex terms, that have room for both positives and negatives. 
This is a view where there’s a teeny bit more context for the splitting, but retaining some sense of how much has-been written between “flips”

I also tried putting them on cards. 

I also tried a book, with a much smaller selection of excerpts printed largely. 


I asked folks to help me decide on a direction, and Alec, Febo and Rosalie came to help. 
  • Alec asked me about my audience for the thing—and that was a challenging set of questions to answer. I’m still struggling with that somewhat. The idea the audience is me isn’t quite right. I think maybe the audience of the pieces themselves are me, but there’s also a more public facing project about the process and the therapeutic factor. There may also be an in between thing I’m craving, around how sharing this with people in my life makes me more legible to others. I also sometimes imagine my mother as the audience. 
  • Rosalie made a connection to fabric crafts, and the idea of hiding and revealing parts of this
  • Febo pointed out that the volume of text and the form are both factors in whether people will read the messages or just kind of gloss over them en masse. I felt a tension around recreating the experience of getting them one by one and the juxtaposition as they alternate, and the desire to dilute them by not having people really read them all. 
  • Alec had an idea about capturing the way they emerge out of the big mass of messages by using a scrolling form with holes in it. I am going to try it, even though I think I am more attached to the accordion book

I asked Rosalie what she thought about having vertical type for the background with a ragged edge, vs horizontal without it. She liked the ragged edge, and felt like it made sense because you can unfold the whole long thing as one “screed” 

Choosing paper and printing was tricky! The first place I tried had crappy quality, but gnomon copy was great! 

I tested a version with light gray background lettering and one with black, and liked the black a bunch better


Accordion finding looks great in theory... 

In practice achieving a clean look is hard! The edges are uneven 

And I made a dent! This was a good moment to stop and reflect. I got to upset! I Leinster a whole backup copy so I wouldn’t freak about mistakes, but because it’s already glued replacing it has the potential to be a lot worse than the dent. I’m torn about whether to try to fix it or just leave it. 

I decided to do a visible mend! First I thought of just patching it, then I decided to use the text paper to patch it instead. 


The thing is coming together, it’s almost a book. 




Graphic critique:
  • The underlines might not have enough breathing room on the sides
  • The black text is dense
  • I didn’t clean up text errors in the “all text"
  • Uneven edges
  • I think the red and blue are effective
  • I like the split front and the solid back
  • I like the heft of it, it speaks to the volume of stuff, and how long it is rolled out
  • It stands up well and does the hologram thing

Idea critique:
  • Heft and length
  • Visible mend
  • juxtaposition
  • Emerging out of the dense mess

Big ideas:
  • I had some insights about the therapeutic factor of this project today. I think it may fit into the “trauma narrative” kind of frame. There’s something about taking control of the narrative, and controlling the dosing of the exposure that is really powerful. I had no control over my experience receiving these emails, and was limited in my ability to make meaning while they were happening, but through this project I am getting to take control and ownership. 
  • There’s also something about becoming legible to other people. I am drawn to bringing others into the process. I think one one level I am the audience for the physical pieces, and on some other level this is a performance 
  • Spending more time with the content today helped me understand this as an emotional processing experience. I may need to read these passages again and again until they lose their potency, and this process forces that, while allowing me to look “sideways" at them because many attention is on craft 
What is good:
  • I think this process is allowing some needed emotional processing for me, and leading to a place where insights about practice are going to be possible
  • I’m loving the design process—every time I work with paper I remember that I love it. I think I like it more than fiber, it’s just not as widely usable
  • I think the object itself is going to be evocative
What is not good:
  • I’m struggling a lot with questions about audience. I’m not exactly sure who the audience for this project is, and it’s really showing up in the process. Alec is asking lots of good questions about how audience impacts whether it’s good and I’m facing back into formalism, which is a weird zone for me. 
  • It’s emotionally shitty to be in this zone for a long time, even though I think it is useful to me 
  • Formal critique vs. idea critique Arte really different zones. There were quite a few times today when I had the thought that if this were a RISD project the mistakes I was making might be unacceptable, but that this setting is different. Is that true?
