--- title: Seeing no Pattern author: Miriam Zisook ---
“Everything has two handles: one by which it may be carried, the other by which it can’t be carried.” 
-Epictetus
“Wabi Sabi is, in one respect, the condition of coming to terms with what you consider ugly.”
-Leonard Koren




When I got the timeline with tags implemented, and stepped back to look at it, I expected to see a pattern. When there appeared to be nothing more than a random smattering of dots I had 2 simultaneous thoughts: 1) I guess I’ll have to keep building more to find the pattern. 2) What if there is no pattern here?

I think that when I asked, “What if there is no pattern?” I was really asking, “What if there is no meaning to be made here?” This is pointing at why this project is meaningful to me, and what it has to do with Brilliant Imperfection. Until I asked that question, I didn’t understand what I was doing: What I’m doing here is looking for a golden thread that when woven through these emails and texts turns them from hurtful chaos into something meaningful. I am digging into these emails out of the hope (or superstition) that if I can make something beautiful out of them, or dig up some pattern in them, I can give them a handle by which they can be carried. I’m mixing metaphors here, trying to tie the stoic Epictetus's handles to the Kintsugi golden scar. It seems to me they are connected by the deeply human wish for the broken and imperfect to be given meaning. Is meaning making “the handle by which it (the hard and heavy stuff of life) can be carried?”
